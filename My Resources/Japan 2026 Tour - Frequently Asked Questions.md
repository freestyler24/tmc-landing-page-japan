## Tour Details

### 1. How does this tour benefit students?

The tour provides holistic growth through social-emotional skill development, cultural learning with deep immersion in Japanese heritage, confidence building by stepping out of familiar environments, academic relevance through visits to iconic sites, peer interaction for relationship-building, global perspective development, and memory-making experiences that last a lifetime.

---

### 2. What cities are covered in this tour?

The 9-day tour covers 4 cities: Tokyo (urban intensity), Mt. Fuji & Hakone (natural wonder), Kyoto (cultural soul), and Nara & Osaka (living history and modern energy).

---

### 3. What are the tour dates?

The tour will take place in October 2026. Exact dates will be confirmed based on batch assignments. The tour is open to students from Grades 6-12.

---

### 4. Which std students will be clubbed together?

Students will be grouped by grade level for optimal peer interaction and age-appropriate activities. Grades 6-8 students will be clubbed together, and Grades 9-12 students will be clubbed together.

---

### 5. What is the maximum group size?

Each batch is limited to a maximum of 45 students to ensure high-quality supervision, personalized attention, and effective group management throughout the tour.

---

### 6. Will there be shopping opportunities during the tour?

Yes. Planned stops include shopping in Tokyo (Shibuya), Kyoto, and Osaka. At Universal Studios, students will have a structured meal allowance system to encourage budgeting and responsibility.

## Safety & Logistics

### 12. What are the safety protocols in place?

Safety measures include a 1:20 teacher ratio with trained tour directors, structured on-ground oversight and comprehensive travel insurance, 4-star hotels with twin sharing rooms, private coach with regulated driving hours as per Japanese law, maximum 45 students per batch for quality supervision, and English-speaking local guides with dedicated tour directors.

---

### 13. What documents are required for visa processing?

Required documents include:

- Valid passport (until May 2027)
- All old passports
- Signed visa form by both parents
- 2 latest passport-size photos (white background, matte finish, 80% face)
- Bank-attested 6-month bank statement
- Last 3 years' IT returns/Form 16
- School ID & bonafide certificate
- Sponsorship letter and supporting financial documents
- Parent's business/occupation proof

Biometric appointment at Visa Application Centre is mandatory.

---

### 14. How will meals be managed?

Students will receive three full meals per day — a combination of Indian, Continental, and Japanese cuisine to ensure variety and familiarity. Dietary restrictions can be accommodated with prior notice.

---

### 15. What kind of hotels and room sharing can be expected?

Students will stay in branded 4-star hotels in twin-sharing rooms (2 students per room), ensuring comfort and safety throughout the tour.

---

### 16. What kind of transportation will be used?

International flights with leading carriers (Singapore Airlines, Malaysian Airlines, Vietnamese Airlines, Japan Airlines), private luxury coach for intercity travel, pre-arranged airport transfers with trusted partners, and the famous Shinkansen bullet train experience are all included.

---

### 17. Can parents contact their children during the trip?

Yes. During the pre-departure orientation, parents will be introduced to communication services enabling smooth international contact. Students will be reachable via tour supervisors and designated communication channels.

### 18. What support is offered for children with special needs?

Please inform us during registration about any special needs. We will work to make all necessary accommodations to ensure your child has a safe and enjoyable experience.
